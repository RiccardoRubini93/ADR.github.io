MUHAMMAD ANAS ABD BARI

University of Southampton Highfield Campus · Southampton, SO17 1BJ, UK · anas.abd-bari@soton.ac.uk

I am currently a PhD candidate in the Aerodynamics and Flight Mechanics group of the University of Southampton. Before joining the research group, I worked as a research assistant on two main projects: one on flapping wing micro-air-vehicles and the other on conceptual aircraft design.

Experience

Research Assistant

University Putra Malaysia

Aerospace Engineering Department, Universiti Putra, Malaysia


Upon graduation from my MEng, I worked as a research assistant on two interesting projects to develop further skills and experience in preparation for my doctorate study. Within the Aerodynamics of Flapping Ornithopter project, I was responsible to carry out analytical analysis, computational fluid dynamics simulation and to develop a physical model of the Ornithopter. The work, which involved investigating unsteady flow features dominated by viscous effect, leading-edge suction and leading edge vortices, was published on the Journal of Aeroelasticity and Structural Dynamics and Applied Mechanics and Materials. Furthermore, I worked on the conceptual design of a Double-Bouble Aircraft concept, published in the International Journal of Mechanical & Mechatronics Engineering.

December 2013 - December 2014

Education

PhD Degree

University of Southampton
Faculty of Engineering and the Environment, University of Southampton, UK.

In my PhD work, I investigate the impact that rain has on the performance of wind turbines, considering structural flexibility effects of the blades. The project is multidisciplinary in its nature, involving multi-phase flows, structural dynamics, and turbulence modelling.

January 2015 - Present

MEng Degree

University of Southampton
Faculty of Engineering and the Environment, University of Southampton, UK.

During my undergraduate years, I was working on two main projects. The first project was the investigation of MILD combustion, a newly discovered type of combustion which is more efficient and produce lesser pollutants in comparison to conventional combustion process. The second project was on balloon launched glider. This project involved designing, manufacturing and testing a Balloon Launched Glider equipped with Lagrangian Ocean Drifter capable of measuring the ocean current velocity at a depth of 10m and relaying the data remotely for a duration of up to three weeks.
