2017 - Present

Doctoral Researcher

University of Glasgow, United Kingdon

Developed and validated a novel three-dimensional massively parallel structured multi-block finite volume CFD system COSA for general fluid-based applications. Carried out detailed complex three-dimensional CFD simulations of unsteady turbulent periodic flows past oscillating wings, multi-megawatt horizontal-axis wind turbines (HAWTs), and vertical-axis wind turbines, on national and international clusters using 10,000s of cores. Written and used very sophisticated interfaces between commercial and research CFD codes, including CGNS. Generated fairly complex three-dimensional computational grids using ANSYS ICEM CFD and grid generators of NUMECA FINE TURBO. Applied the non-linear frequency-domain harmonic balance technology to complex yawed flow aerodynamics of HAWTs. Demonstrated that HAWT periodic flows can be computed by the harmonic balance solver about fifty times more rapidly than by the conventional time-domain analysis, with accuracy comparable to that of the time-domain solver.
